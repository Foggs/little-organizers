Show me more!

Hi my name is Ann Marie Little. I have been a little organizer ever since I was a kid growing up in New York and Pennsylvania. As a child, I found great comfort in things being orderly so I often found myself organizing every room, drawer, cabinet, and closet –not only my house but my friends' houses also!

When things feel out of control, we often don’t know where to begin. I love to put things in sections and match them with other common items, while also arranging items in height order or color coded. To me, things not only have to look pretty once they are sorted, they also need to make sense and therefore make life easier.

I also love to tackle big projects that most people find intimidating, such as the garage, shed, attic and basement. It gives me great pleasure to sort through everything and create order out of chaos.

Although I have a love for organizing I did not choose that as my career path out of high school. I decided to pursue a degree in Accounting and soon found myself with a high-paced job at a Manhattan firm. I started at the bottom and, through hard work and dedication, I climbed my way up the corporate ladder.

But even with a busy a career and a young family, I continued to organize houses, apartments, dorm rooms and offices on the side. I did this because it made me happy. Eventually, I decided to turn in my briefcase and pursue my dream of becoming an entrepreneur.

The result is Little Organizers, a small business dedicated to helping clients organize their lives in a way that not only makes sense but is also easy to use and simple to maintain.

AnnMarie Little

Bedrooms

The key to a well put together bedroom is an expertly organized closet. When it comes to closets, I just love to make the best use of the space you have. I like to make color-coded sections in the closet and separate work, dress and casual clothing, all on matching hangers. An important rule for closet organization is visibility. I believe if you can’t see something, you will rip your closet apart looking for it. That’s why I like to organize sweaters, scarves, belts, wallets, purses, and shoes in clear bins. Another great joy of mine is to organize drawers, with separate compartments for socks and undergarments. It not only makes putting your clothes away easier but also makes finding them a piece of cake. Additionally, I can assist you in editing your wardrobe to discard, donate or sell unwanted and outdated items. I can also clear off all the clutter on top of the dressers and make everything look beautiful and orderly.

Basement and Attic
(finished or unfinished)

This is typically the dumping ground for many items that we either use infrequently/seasonally or not at all. Worse than that, they are usually not stored or protected properly. I absolutely love to tackle these huge projects. I take every single item and separate it into categories, and then either with the purchase of bins or using what you have, I protect and pack the items and store them in categories. If there is a need for shelving I not only make the purchase, but I assemble and install them as well. How nice will it be to go into these areas and easily find what you are looking for? Also, protection is key in these areas as they tend to be most susceptible to climate, rodents, and possible leaks. It’s not a pleasant experience when your pipe bursts and photographs that were not protected properly are damaged and priceless memories are lost. On a more positive note, I have had clients discover things that they didn’t even know they had -- everything from an extra coffee pot to lost photographs to forgotten money. There could be so many treasures just waiting to be found and sometimes the discovery of lost items ends up paying for the services themselves.

Garage

This usually is another collection space in our homes for everything from tools to sports equipment to seasonal decorations to items we no longer use. How many of us have a garage so poorly organized that we have room for pretty much everything except the car! I like to clear the entire garage out if possible and categorize all items in piles outside on the property. At this point, we will also identify items that can be discarded, donated or sold. Next, I give the empty garage a good sweep and purchase and assemble any shelving required. I then pack everything up using labeled bins if necessary and find the perfect spot for each bin. Care is given to create a system that is easy to follow, saves space and offers the protection that comes from proper storage. After everything is said and done, you will have a space that functions both as a storage space AND as a garage for your car. That’s right… you really can have it all!

Pre-move

Whether you are downsizing or upgrading, I can assist in preparing for a move of any kind. There are several reasons it is important to properly prepare for a move. Not only can it save you money on the actual move itself, but it can also make unpacking a much simpler process. No matter how much we may look forward to moving to a new home, the stress that surrounds packing is undeniable because the task can seem so daunting and intimidating. But preparing for your upcoming move can also offer you an opportunity to organize your belongings like never before. We can go through everything and identify what items are not worth taking or that you no longer want or need and either discard, donate or sell them before the move therefore saving you money with the moving company and potentially making a bit of money to offset some of the moving costs. All items that are worthy for the move will be packed safely and in clearly marked boxes designated for each room of the new house and making the unpacking process much easier. It is also nice to pack “immediate use” boxes so that the important items you need for that day (such as sheets and toiletries) will be clearly marked and prioritized. Don’t just pack -- pack in an organized fashion!

Prepare a home for a renovation

Whether it be finishing the basement, redesigning the kitchen or gutting the entire home I can help make this process less complicated. We can take this opportunity to go through all items and see what we can discard, donate or sell and then take the items that we decide to keep and pack them properly in marked boxes and move them either in a pod or another area of the house. Attention will be paid to make sure your items are stored in a way that will protect them from the inevitable dust and debris that comes with renovations. After the construction has taken place I can come back and help you unpack all boxes, consolidate the boxes and packing materials for curbside pickup and put all items away in an organized fashion. Having a clear space to work in will allow your contractor to complete the project more quickly and can help keep the crew on schedule. This will also give you more time to spend overseeing the work, and picking out all your new items for the project.
